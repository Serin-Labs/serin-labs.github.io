Before You Buy

Will it work with my Mitsubishi unit?

It works with most Mitsubishi units that have an internal CN105 connector — wall-mount, floor, cassette, ducted, and P-series, plus rebranded Trane and American Standard units. Type your model number into the compatibility checker for an instant answer. Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (SRK/SRC/SRF) and Ecodan air-to-water units are not compatible.

What's included with the pre-assembled controller?

The $35 Serin Controller includes the controller and a Mitsubishi CN105-compatible cable — everything needed to plug into the indoor unit. An optional wireless remote temperature sensor is available at checkout. You'll also need a USB-C data cable for the one-time firmware flash.

Does it ship with firmware installed?

No. The controller arrives without firmware so you pick your platform: flash the free open-source ESPHome or HomeKit-compatible firmware from the browser flash tool in about five minutes.

HomeKit or ESPHome — which should I choose?

Use the HomeKit-compatible firmware if your home runs on Apple Home and Siri; use ESPHome if you run Home Assistant. Both are free, both are local, and you can reflash to switch anytime. Full comparison: ESPHome vs HomeKit.

How many controllers do I need?

One per indoor head — each indoor unit has its own CN105 port. A 3-zone system needs three controllers.

How does it compare to the MHK2 or Kumo Cloud?

The $35 controller does what the $180–$400 MHK2 wall controller and the Kumo Cloud adapter do — full mode, temperature, fan, and vane control — but locally, with no cloud account and no subscription. See every option compared.

Setup & Firmware

What do I need to flash the firmware?

A desktop Chromium browser (Chrome, Edge, or Brave — Safari and Firefox don't support Web Serial) and a USB-C data cable. The flash tool runs entirely in your browser; nothing to install.

How do firmware updates work? Is there a subscription?

Updates are free and open source — no subscription, ever. After the first USB flash they arrive over Wi-Fi: ESPHome pushes them from your dashboard, and the HomeKit-compatible firmware updates from its built-in web UI. Details on the support page.

Does it need the internet to work?

No. Control is local on your network. For control away from home you use your platform's own remote access: an Apple Home hub (Apple TV or HomePod) for HomeKit, or Home Assistant remote access for ESPHome.
