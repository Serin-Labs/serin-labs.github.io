HomeKit-Compatible Setup

In about 15 minutes, your Mitsubishi mini-split answers to the Apple Home app and Siri — no monthly fees, no cloud account, no Home Assistant. The firmware is free and open-source, and you flash it yourself right from your browser.

  1. 1Connect to your unit
  2. 2Join Wi-Fi
  3. 3Add to Apple Home
  4. ✓Done

You're controlling your unit from your phone after Step 3 — about 15 minutes. Step 4 and everything below it (web UI, updates, troubleshooting) is optional reference. See the full feature list on the features page.

Your device, , was flashed successfully. Steps 2, 3 & 4 below have custom QR codes ready to scan — connect it to Wi-Fi, add it to Apple Home, and open its web UI.

You'll need

  • Your Serin Controller (or an ESP32 board with a CN105 cable)
  • A screwdriver to open the indoor unit's cover
  • An iPhone or iPad, and your home Wi-Fi password
  • For remote access & automations, an Apple Home Hub (Apple TV or HomePod)

Good to know

  • Pairs with the Apple Home app and Siri through the HomeKit Accessory Protocol (HAP). It's not MFi-certified and isn't an Apple-certified accessory — it implements HAP independently.
  • No crimping or soldering — the pre-assembled controller and ready-made cables plug straight into the CN105 port.

1. Connect to Your HVAC Unit

  1. Turn off power to your HVAC unit and open the cover to locate the CN105 connector (usually red, sometimes white).
  2. Plug the CN105 connector into the HVAC unit and Grove connector into the device.
  3. Place the device safely inside the unit if needed, reinstall the cover, and restore power.

Don't have the hardware yet? Get the pre-assembled Serin Controller with the cable included, or grab a ready-made CN105-to-Grove cable on its own — assembled and tested, no crimping required.

2. Connect to WiFi

serinlabs
Scan this QR code to join the device's hotspot. A portal opens for you to enter your home Wi-Fi credentials. Once it connects, continue to Step 3.

On first boot (or after 5 minutes without Wi-Fi), the device creates a Wi-Fi access point:

  1. Connect to the Serin-XXXX network (XXXX = last 4 characters of the device's WiFi address, password: serinlabs).
  2. A setup page opens. Enter your home WiFi name and password.
    • If it doesn't open automatically, go to http://192.168.4.1 in a browser.
  3. The device saves your credentials and reboots.
Connect over USB instead (Improv Serial)

On a computer using Chrome or Edge, plug the device in over USB to set up Wi-Fi straight from this page — pick your network, enter the password, done. No hotspot needed. (Not available on phones or in Safari/Firefox, which lack Web Serial.)

Web Serial isn't available in this browser — use desktop Chrome or Edge.

3. Add to Apple Home

XXX-XX-XXX
Scan this QR code to add the device to Apple Home. If successful, you're done with set up!

Option A: Scan QR Code (recommended)

  1. In a browser, open the device's web UI at http://Serin-XXXX.local (XXXX = the suffix from Step 2). If .local doesn't resolve, use http://<device-ip> from your router.
  2. Scan the QR code shown in the HomeKit panel with your iPhone or iPad.

Option B: Manual Setup Code

  1. Open the Home app on your iPhone or iPad.
  2. Tap + > Add Accessory.
  3. Tap More options… and select Mini Split XXXX.
  4. Enter the setup code shown in the web UI's HomeKit panel.

4. Access Web UI

Open in browser
Serin-XXXX.local
Scan this QR code to open the device's web UI in your browser.

The built-in web UI is reachable at http://Serin-XXXX.local or http://<device-ip>. It provides:

  • Thermostat control (mode, temperature, fan speed)
  • Vane control (vertical and wide vane positions)
  • Diagnostics (compressor frequency, outside temp, error codes)
  • Settings (device name, poll interval, temperature unit)
  • Real-time log streaming
Updating the firmware later (OTA)

Update firmware over the air without USB access:

  • Check for Updates (easiest): In the web UI, open Settings > Firmware Update and tap Check for Updates. If a newer release is available, tap Install. The device downloads it, verifies the SHA256 checksum, and restarts. No manual download needed.
  • Manual upload: Upload a firmware .bin in the same Firmware Update section. The browser verifies integrity before uploading.
  • Via curl:
idf.py build
curl --data-binary @build/mitsubishi-cn105-homekit.bin \
     -H "Content-Type: application/octet-stream" \
     http://<device-ip>/upload

After an OTA update, the device validates WiFi and UART communication before confirming the new firmware. If validation fails, it automatically rolls back.

Troubleshooting & Wi-Fi recovery

WiFi Recovery

MethodDetails
Auto hotspotThe device creates a fallback hotspot after 5 minutes without WiFi. It turns off once WiFi reconnects.
Recovery pageConnect to the hotspot, then go to 192.168.4.1 to enter new WiFi credentials.
Button resetHold the board button for 10 seconds to erase stored WiFi credentials.

Re-pairing

If you need to re-pair to Apple Home, open the web UI and use the "Reset Pairing" button in the HomeKit panel, then add the device again from the Home app.
